\subsection*{Citizen Radar Science}

Speed data is collected using a Doppler radar sensor (OmniPreSense OPS243-A)
mounted at the roadside. The radar measures the radial component of each
vehicle's velocity. When the sensor is mounted at an angle $\theta$ to the
traffic flow, the measured speed $v_m$ relates to the true speed $v$ by:
\[
  v = \frac{v_m}{\cos(\theta)}
\]

\textbf{Aggregation method:} observations are grouped into <<.Group>>
intervals. Within each interval, percentile speeds (P50, P85, P98) are
calculated over the population of individual vehicle maximum speeds.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{P50} (median): half of vehicles travel slower than this speed.
  \item \textbf{P85}: 85\% of vehicles travel slower. Used by traffic
        engineers as the ``design speed'' for road safety.
  \item \textbf{P98}: 98\% of vehicles travel slower. Identifies habitual
        speeders.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Kinetic energy:} a vehicle's kinetic energy is $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
Pedestrian fatality risk rises steeply with impact speed: at 30~mph the risk
is approximately 10\%; at 40~mph it exceeds 50\%.
